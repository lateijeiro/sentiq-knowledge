# Sentiq B2C User Acquisition

Version: 1.1

Status: Strategy + Current channels + Planned scale

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Sentiq acquires individual athletes.

The objective is attracting athletes who are likely to build a real development habit — not maximizing downloads.

---

# Acquisition Philosophy

Sentiq should grow through trust.

Athletes trust coaches, teammates, clubs and sports communities more than ads.

Therefore the strongest channels are relationship-based.

---

# Acquisition Principles

## 1. Quality Over Volume

A smaller group of engaged athletes is more valuable than thousands of inactive users.

Priority order: activation → retention → love → scale.

## 2. Community Before Scale

Sports communities already exist. Sentiq should integrate into them.

## 3. Organic Growth First

Before heavy paid acquisition, prove:

- Athletes understand the value
- Athletes return
- Athletes recommend it

---

# Current Acquisition Channels

---

## Channel 1 — Clubs, Coaches And Organizations (Current)

Coaches are trusted development figures.

Strategy:

- Rugby clubs, academies and youth categories
- Sentiq Org as the operational layer for clubs
- Organization invitations that bring athletes into the app

Measurement:

- Athletes invited
- Registrations
- Activation (first evaluation / first objective)
- Retention

Note: org invitations may also grant Pro through organization membership. That is B2B-assisted B2C acquisition, not athlete referral rewards.

---

## Channel 2 — Partner And Event Campaigns (Current)

Shared campaign codes distribute time-limited or campaign-defined Pro access.

Use cases:

- Partner launches
- Camps and tournaments
- Club pilots with capacity limits

Athletes redeem through the campaign landing flow. The mobile app shows campaign Pro access on Home when applicable.

See [campaigns.md](./campaigns.md).

Campaigns are not personal referral codes.

---

## Channel 3 — Early Adopters (Current)

First-100 validation cohort with lifetime early-adopter recognition and Pro access when assigned.

See [early-adopters.md](./early-adopters.md).

---

## Channel 4 — Content Marketing (Current)

Educational content creates awareness before conversion.

Primary surfaces:

- Website (sentiq-front)
- Instagram
- LinkedIn
- Blog / product pages

Content themes:

- Athlete development
- Reflection
- Confidence
- Consistency
- Leadership and team culture

---

## Channel 5 — Athlete Ambassadors (Strategy / early)

Identify competitive athletes, youth leaders and club references who share authentic journeys.

This is relationship-led, not a formal reward system yet.

---

## Channel 6 — Red Invites (Current, weak acquisition loop)

Athletes can invite others to follow them inside Red.

This strengthens community and peer support.

It does **not** currently:

- Attribute acquisition to an inviter
- Grant Pro for invites
- Function as a referral rewards program

See [referrals.md](./referrals.md).

---

# Planned Acquisition Channels

## Athlete Referral Program (Planned)

Personalized invite links or codes with attribution and quality-first incentives.

See [referrals.md](./referrals.md).

## Paid Acquisition (Planned after retention proof)

Possible later:

- Instagram / TikTok / Google ads
- Sponsored sports community placements

Do not scale paid before retention and activation quality are proven.

# Acquisition Funnel (Current)

```
Awareness
   ↓
Interest
   ↓
Download / Register
   ↓
Onboarding (profile, mode, schedule)
   ↓
First Evaluation
   ↓
First Objective
   ↓
Consistency (return + notifications)
   ↓
Pro (purchase, campaign, unlock, org, pilot)
```

---

# Activation Definition (Current)

A user is activated when they experience core value.

Recommended activation event:

- First evaluation completed
- First objective created

Onboarding completion alone is not activation.

---

# Acquisition Metrics

## Awareness

Website visits, social reach, content engagement, community mentions.

## Acquisition

Downloads, registrations, source attribution (campaign code, org invite, organic, etc.).

## Activation

Onboarding completion, first evaluation, first objective.

## Retention

WAU / MAU, evaluation frequency.

## Referral / Invite (split)

Current:

- Red invites sent / accepted
- Campaign joins
- Org invitations accepted

Planned:

- Personal referral conversions
- Referred-user retention quality

---

# Acquisition Risks

Avoid:

- Vanity growth (downloads without usage)
- Wrong audience (users not interested in development)
- Growth before value
- Treating Red follows as referral success
- Treating campaign codes as athlete referral attribution

---

# Success Criteria

A successful acquisition creates athletes who say:

"I discovered Sentiq and it became part of my development."

Not:

"I downloaded another sports app."

---

# Final Principle

The best acquisition channel for Sentiq is a recommendation from someone who already believes in athlete development.
