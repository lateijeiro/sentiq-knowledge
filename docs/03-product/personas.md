# Sentiq Product Personas

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the primary users of Sentiq.

Every product decision should consider:

- Who is this helping?
- What problem are we solving?
- What behavior are we trying to create?

Sentiq is built around people, not features.

---

# Persona 1 — The Athlete

## Overview

The athlete is the primary user of Sentiq.

A young person who wants to improve in their sport and understand how to become better over time.

Uses **sentiq-app**.

The athlete is not necessarily looking for technology.

They are looking for progress.

---

## Profile

Typical characteristics:

- Age: 14–23 years old
- Sport: any sport; priority early outreach includes rugby, hockey, football and basketball
- Profile role in app: jugador, coach, or otro (persona label, not org role)

---

## Context

The athlete:

- Trains several times per week
- Receives technical coaching
- Wants to improve performance
- Experiences wins and losses
- Faces pressure and competition
- Is developing their identity

Sport is an important part of their life.

---

## Main Goals

The athlete wants to:

- Improve performance
- Become more consistent
- Gain confidence
- Understand strengths and weaknesses
- Set and achieve personal objectives
- Feel progress

---

## Hidden Needs

The athlete may not explicitly say:

"I need self-awareness."

"I need reflection."

But they feel:

"I want to improve."

"I want to know if I am getting better."

"I want to perform when it matters."

---

## Frustrations

The athlete struggles with:

- Not knowing what to improve
- Losing confidence after mistakes
- Inconsistent performance
- Difficulty maintaining focus
- Lack of feedback between training sessions
- Feeling that progress is invisible

---

## What Sentiq Provides

Through sentiq-app:

- Event-based evaluations (full wizard and quick check-in)
- Five-dimension self-assessment after training and matches
- Personal objectives with AI suggestions and Meta Foco alignment
- Progress visibility through IEA, trends and streaks
- Coach Sentiq outputs: objective suggestions, preparation, monthly report, Learning Cards
- Follower network (Red) for peer support

---

## Athlete Success Moment

The athlete thinks:

"I understand myself better."

"I know what I want to improve."

"I can see my progress."

---

## Athlete Fear

The athlete does not want:

- More homework
- Another complicated app
- More pressure
- Judgment
- Comparison with teammates

---

## Product Implications

For athletes, the experience must be:

- Fast (quick evaluation when time is limited)
- Simple (one primary action per screen)
- Personal (privacy control per evaluation)
- Positive (no judgment, no failure framing)
- Rewarding (streaks, badges, IEA progress)

---

# Persona 2 — The Coach / Staff

## Overview

The coach or staff member is responsible for guiding athlete development at the organization level.

Uses **sentiq-org**.

Sentiq does not replace the coach.

It makes the coach more effective through visibility and conversation starters.

---

## Profile

Typical characteristics:

- Coach, youth development coach or academy coach across any sport
- Organization role: coach or staff (Membership.role)

---

## Context

The coach manages:

- Multiple athletes across rosters and teams
- Different personalities and development stages
- Limited time for individual conversations

The coach wants to support every athlete but cannot always have deep conversations with everyone.

---

## Main Goals

The coach wants to:

- Develop better athletes
- Understand players better between training sessions
- Have meaningful conversations backed by data
- Identify athletes who need attention
- Monitor team emotional climate and development trends

---

## Frustrations

The coach struggles with:

- Limited visibility outside training
- Forgetting individual details
- Difficulty tracking long-term progress
- Communication gaps
- Athletes not expressing concerns

---

## What Sentiq Provides

Through sentiq-org:

- **Pulse Dashboard**: evaluation volume, emotional climate, objective completion, IEA readiness, attention alerts
- **Player profiles**: evaluation history, reflection, objectives, IEA and trends
- **Reports**: weekly briefing, adoption ROI
- **Structure**: roster and team management, staff assignments

---

## Coach Success Moment

The coach thinks:

"Now I understand my players better."

"This helps me have better conversations."

"I can see who needs attention this week."

---

## Coach Fear

The coach does not want:

- Technology replacing intuition
- Extra administrative work
- Complex dashboards
- Another obligation

---

## Product Implications

For coaches:

- Insights must be actionable, not just data
- Information must lead to conversations
- Dashboard must save time, not create work
- No ranking or comparison between players

---

# Persona 3 — The Club / Academy Admin

## Overview

The club or academy is the organization that creates the environment where athletes develop.

Uses **sentiq-org** with club_admin role.

Sentiq helps clubs create a stronger development culture at scale.

---

## Profile

Examples:

- Rugby, hockey, football and basketball clubs
- Sports academies
- Schools with sports programs
- Organizations from any other sport

Organization role: club_admin

---

## Context

Organizations manage:

- Multiple rosters and teams
- Multiple coaches and staff
- Many athletes
- Different development approaches

They need consistency and visibility across the organization.

---

## Main Goals

The organization wants to:

- Improve athlete development systematically
- Retain players
- Support and align coaches
- Create a visible development culture
- Demonstrate impact to stakeholders
- Manage billing and organization limits

---

## Frustrations

Organizations struggle with:

- Development depending too much on individual coaches
- Lack of structured processes
- Limited visibility across teams
- Difficulty demonstrating impact

---

## What Sentiq Provides

Through sentiq-org:

- Organization-wide Pulse Dashboard with scoped filters
- Player management: invitations (single, bulk, CSV), roster/team assignment
- Staff management: roles, assignments to rosters/teams
- Organization reports: weekly briefing, adoption ROI
- Settings and billing: profile, limits, plan usage, invoices

---

## Club Success Moment

The club thinks:

"Sentiq helps us develop athletes better."

"It aligns our coaches."

"We can see development happening across the organization."

---

## Product Implications

For clubs:

- Reliable and simple to deploy
- Easy for coaches to adopt
- Clearly valuable for development methodology
- Scalable across rosters and teams

---

# Persona Priority

The product priority order is:

```
1. Athlete (sentiq-app)
2. Coach / Staff (sentiq-org)
3. Club / Academy Admin (sentiq-org)
```

This order should guide product decisions.

---

# Final Persona Principle

Sentiq is built for athletes.

Coaches amplify the impact through Sentiq Org.

Clubs scale the impact through organization tools.
