# Sentiq Overview

Version: 1.0

---

# Executive Summary

Sentiq is a digital platform designed to help young athletes develop greater self-awareness, consistency and confidence through structured reflection and continuous personal development.

Rather than measuring only physical performance, Sentiq focuses on the part of sports that is rarely trained systematically:

How athletes think.

How they learn.

How they respond to adversity.

How they build consistency.

The platform transforms personal development into a simple routine tied to training and competition.

---

# Why Sentiq Exists

Modern sports dedicate thousands of hours to improving physical performance.

Very little time is invested in helping athletes understand themselves.

Young athletes often receive technical coaching but rarely develop the skills required to manage pressure, frustration, confidence, motivation or long-term growth.

Many promising athletes fail because of factors unrelated to talent.

Sentiq exists to close that gap.

Its mission is not to replace coaches or psychologists.

Its mission is to make personal development part of everyday training.

---

# The Problem

Most athlete development programs are heavily focused on:

- Strength
- Conditioning
- Technique
- Tactics

While almost no structured system exists for helping athletes develop:

- Self-awareness
- Reflection
- Emotional regulation
- Consistency
- Confidence
- Accountability

These areas are usually addressed informally.

Sometimes they are ignored completely.

As a result:

Athletes struggle to identify patterns.

Coaches lack objective information.

Development becomes reactive instead of intentional.

---

# Our Hypothesis

Small moments of reflection performed consistently produce better long-term athletes.

Not because reflection alone improves performance.

But because awareness improves decision making.

Better decisions improve consistency.

Better consistency improves performance.

---

This distinction is fundamental.

The platform focuses on development.

Not diagnosis.

Growth.

Not treatment.

Learning.

Not therapy.

Everything inside Sentiq should reinforce this positioning.

# Current Product Systems

## Athlete App (sentiq-app)

- **Evaluations**: full wizard and quick check-in after training, matches or other events
- **Objectives**: personal development actions with priority, category, and Meta Foco alignment
- **Meta Foco**: medium-term development goal above individual objectives
- **Progress**: KPIs, trends, streaks, IEA, monthly AI report, Learning Cards (Pro)
- **Red**: follower network with shared evaluations and objective suggestions
- **Sports Modes**: competencia, construccion, ajuste, off — drive notifications and preparation
- **Coach Sentiq**: AI-generated objective suggestions, weekly preparation, monthly report, Learning Cards
- **Pro**: subscription via RevenueCat, early adopter benefits, unlock programs, campaigns

## Organization Portal (sentiq-org)

- **Pulse Dashboard**: team emotional climate, evaluation volume, objective completion, IEA readiness, attention alerts
- **Players**: directory, invitations, individual profiles with evaluation history and IEA
- **Staff & Structure**: roles, rosters, teams, assignments
- **Reports**: weekly briefing, adoption ROI
- **Settings & Billing**: organization profile, limits, invoices

## Backend (sentiq-back)

Express API with MongoDB. Handles all athlete and organization data, AI generation, push notifications, subscriptions, and admin KPIs.

## Marketing Site (sentiq-front)

Public Astro site with product information, blog, pilot access, and legal pages.

## KPI Panel (sentiq-kpi-panel)

Internal admin dashboard for product health, engagement, retention, and monetization metrics.

# Product Principles

Every feature should answer at least one of these questions.

Does it increase self-awareness?

Does it improve consistency?

Does it help athletes learn?

Does it improve conversations between coaches and players?

Does it encourage long-term growth?

If the answer is "no",

the feature probably doesn't belong in Sentiq.

---

# Core Experience

The experience should feel lightweight.

Users should never feel they are "doing homework."

Instead, interactions should become part of their training routine.

Open.

Reflect.

Answer.

Learn.

Continue training.

The product should disappear into the athlete's routine.
