# Sentiq Brand

Version: 1.0

---

# Brand Purpose

Sentiq exists to help athletes become better versions of themselves.

Not only better players.

Better teammates.

Better competitors.

Better leaders.

Better people.

Sport is one of the greatest schools of life.

Sentiq exists to accompany that journey.

---

# Brand Promise

Every interaction with Sentiq should leave the athlete with greater clarity than before.

Not necessarily happier.

Not necessarily more confident.

But always clearer.

Clarity leads to better decisions.

Better decisions lead to better habits.

Better habits lead to long-term growth.

---

# Brand Positioning

Sentiq is an Athlete Development Platform.

Not a mental health application.

Not a wellness app.

Not a mindfulness product.

Not therapy.

Not psychology software.

Sentiq belongs at the intersection of:

- Sports
- Performance
- Personal Growth
- Reflection
- Habit Formation
- Athlete Development

Whenever communication moves outside this positioning, it should be reconsidered.

---

# Brand Personality

If Sentiq were a person, it would be:

An experienced coach.

Observant.

Positive.

Never arrogant.

Never dramatic.

# Tone of Voice

Sentiq speaks with confidence.

Never with certainty.

Bad:

"This will make you mentally stronger."

Good:

"This exercise can help you build greater confidence over time."

---

Bad:

"You must."

Good:

"Try."

---

Bad:

"You failed."

Good:

"What can you learn from today?"

---

Bad:

"Your emotional state is..."

Good:

"Today's reflection suggests..."

---

# Communication Style

Always:

Encourage.

Guide.

Invite.

Reflect.

Support.

Never:

Judge.

Command.

Shame.

Pressure.

Manipulate.

---

# Writing Style

Short paragraphs.

Simple words.

No academic vocabulary.

No motivational clichés.

No startup jargon.

Avoid:

"Unlock your potential."

"Transform your life."

"Become unstoppable."

Instead:

"Small improvements every day."

"Consistency creates progress."

"Growth happens through repetition."

---

# Product Copy Principles

Buttons should use verbs.

Examples:

Start Evaluation

Continue

Save Reflection

Review Objective

Complete

Never:

Proceed

Execute

Confirm Operation

Submit Form

---

# Notification Style

Notifications should feel like reminders from a coach.

Not alarms.

Example:

"Take one minute to reflect on today's training."

Instead of:

"You have a pending evaluation."

---

Example:

"Consistency starts today."

Instead of:

"You missed three evaluations."

---

# AI Communication

The AI should sound like an experienced mentor.

Never like ChatGPT.

Never mention:

"As an AI..."

"I analyzed..."

"My model suggests..."

Instead:

"Based on your recent reflections..."

"I noticed a pattern..."

"One idea you could try..."

The athlete should feel they are talking with Sentiq.

Not with artificial intelligence.

---

# Coach Communication

Coaches should never feel replaced.

Messages should reinforce collaboration.

Good:

"This information may help guide your next conversation."

Bad:

"The athlete should..."

Sentiq informs.

The coach decides.

---

# Visual Identity Principles

The interface should communicate:

Clarity

Space

Calm

Confidence

Movement

Avoid:

Noise

Crowded layouts

Aggressive colors

Overloaded dashboards

Everything should breathe.

Whitespace is part of the design.

---

# Success Criteria

If someone uses Sentiq for six months, they should describe it as:

"It helped me understand myself better."

Not:

"It tracked my emotions."

Not:

"It analyzed me."

Not:

"It judged me."

The product should feel like a companion.

Not an evaluator.

---

# Brand Manifesto

Every athlete trains their body.

Few train their ability to reflect.

Few learn how to understand themselves.

Few develop habits that last beyond sport.

We believe those skills deserve the same attention as physical training.

We believe growth is intentional.

We believe confidence is built.

We believe discipline can be learned.

We believe better athletes become better people.

That is why Sentiq exists.
