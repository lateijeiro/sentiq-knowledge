# Sentiq B2B Sales Process

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the commercial process for acquiring sports organizations as Sentiq B2B customers.

The objective is to create a repeatable process to identify, contact, onboard and retain clubs and organizations.

---

# Sales Philosophy

Sentiq should not sell software.

The commercial conversation should focus on athlete development.

The objective is not:

"We have a platform for clubs."

The objective is:

"We help clubs create better athlete development processes."

---

# Ideal Customer Profile

The ideal Sentiq B2B customer is an organization that:

- Values athlete development.
- Has youth categories.
- Has committed coaches.
- Wants to improve methodology.
- Is open to innovation.

---

# Priority Customers

Initial focus:

## Rugby Clubs

Reasons:

- Strong organizational culture.
- Structured youth development.

- Coach influence.
- Community relationships.

---

# Stage 1

# Prospect Identification

Objective:

Build a qualified list of organizations.

---

## Information To Collect

Organization:

- Name.
- Sport.
- Location.
- Size.
- Categories.
- Youth programs.
- Contact information.

---

## Priority Signals

Higher priority organizations:

- Strong youth structure.
- Competitive programs.
- Development focus.
- Active social presence.
- Professional organization.

---

# Stage 2

# Initial Contact

Objective:

Generate interest and start a conversation.

---

# Contact Channels

Possible channels:

- Personal network.
- Email.
- LinkedIn.
- Coaches.
- Club references.
- Events.

---

# Communication Principle

The first contact should focus on the problem.

Avoid starting with features.

---

# Example Positioning

"We are working with clubs to improve athlete development through reflection, objectives and better coach-athlete conversations."

---

# Stage 3

# Discovery Conversation

Objective:

Understand the organization's reality.

---

# Key Questions

## About The Club

- How do you currently work on athlete development?
- How do coaches follow individual progress?
- How do athletes participate in their own development?

---

## About Challenges

- What is difficult today?
- Where do coaches need more information?
- How do you measure athlete evolution?

---

## About Adoption

- Who would be involved?
- Which categories could start?
- What would success look like?

---

# Stage 4

# Product Demonstration

Objective:

Show how Sentiq solves real problems.

---

# Demo Focus

The demo should show:

- Athlete experience.
- Evaluations.
- Objectives.
- Evolution.
- Coach value.
- Club implementation.

---

# Demo Principle

Do not demonstrate everything.

Demonstrate what matters for that organization.

---

# Stage 5

# Pilot Proposal

Objective:

Reduce adoption risk.

---

# Pilot Characteristics

A pilot should define:

- Duration.
- Categories involved.
- Number of athletes.
- Coaches involved.
- Success metrics.

---

# Pilot Goals

Validate:

- Athlete adoption.
- Coach usage.
- Product value.
- Implementation process.

---

# Stage 6

# Implementation

Objective:

Create successful adoption.

---

# Implementation Steps

## Organization Setup

Configure:

- Club.
- Categories.
- Coaches.
- Athletes.

---

## Coach Introduction

Explain:

- Why Sentiq exists.
- How it helps.
- How to use insights.

---

## Athlete Activation

Support:

- Registration.
- First evaluation.
- First objective.

---

# Stage 7

# Expansion

Objective:

Grow inside the organization.

---

# Expansion Opportunities

Examples:

- More categories.
- More teams.
- More coaches.
- Additional programs.

---

# Sales Materials

Required assets:

- Sentiq presentation.
- Product demo.
- Pilot proposal.
- Case studies.
- Testimonials.

---

# Sales Metrics

Measure:

## Pipeline

- Organizations contacted.
- Conversations started.
- Demos completed.
- Pilots started.

---

## Conversion

Measure:

- Contact to meeting.
- Meeting to pilot.
- Pilot to customer.

---

## Revenue

Measure:

- Paying clubs.
- Monthly recurring revenue.
- Expansion revenue.

---

# Sales Risks

---

## Selling Features Instead Of Value

Risk:

The club does not understand the strategic benefit.

---

## No Internal Champion

Risk:

Implementation loses momentum.

---

## Too Much Friction

Risk:

Complex onboarding prevents adoption.

---

# Long-Term Sales Vision

Sentiq should build a relationship-driven sales model.

The strongest acquisition channel should become:

Successful clubs recommending Sentiq to other organizations.

---

# Final Principle

The goal of Sentiq sales is not to sell a subscription.

It is to help organizations begin a better way of developing athletes.
