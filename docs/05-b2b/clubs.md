# Sentiq B2B Clubs Strategy

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the role of sports clubs within the Sentiq B2B ecosystem.

The objective is to explain how Sentiq helps clubs improve athlete development through technology, reflection and continuous improvement.

---

# Club Philosophy

A club is not only a place where athletes train.

A club is an environment where athletes develop.

Sentiq helps clubs strengthen their development process by creating a structured connection between:

- Athlete.
- Coach.
- Organization.

---

# The Club Challenge

Sports clubs usually have strong technical, physical and competitive development processes.

However, many clubs lack tools to understand the complete athlete journey.

---

# Main Challenges

## Limited Individual Visibility

Coaches work with multiple athletes.

It is difficult to understand:

- How each athlete feels.
- What challenges each athlete faces.
- What each athlete wants to improve.
- How each athlete perceives their own progress.

---

## Development Depends On Individuals

Many clubs have excellent coaches.

However, athlete development methodology often depends on:

- Specific coaches.
- Personal experience.
- Individual approaches.

Sentiq helps create consistency across the organization.

---

## Communication Gaps

Important conversations between athletes and coaches do not always happen.

Examples:

- Confidence.
- Personal objectives.
- Difficult moments.
- Areas for improvement.

---

## Lack Of Development Data

Most clubs have information about:

- Attendance.
- Competition results.
- Physical performance.

However, they often lack visibility into:

- Athlete perception.
- Personal objectives.
- Development evolution.
- Engagement.

---

# Sentiq Club Value Proposition

Sentiq helps clubs:

- Understand athletes better.
- Improve coach-athlete conversations.
- Create development habits.
- Give athletes ownership of their growth.
- Build a consistent development culture.

---

The coach remains the central figure in athlete development.

Sentiq provides support by improving:

# Club Ecosystem

The Sentiq ecosystem has three main actors.

---

# Athlete

The athlete:

- Completes evaluations.
- Reflects about performance.
- Defines objectives.
- Tracks personal evolution.

---

# Coach

The coach:

- Understands athlete development needs.
- Uses insights during conversations.
- Supports improvement processes.

---

# Organization

The club:

- Defines methodology.
- Encourages adoption.
- Builds a development culture.

# Initial Market Focus

Sentiq is designed for clubs and organizations in any sport.

---

# Priority Outreach Sports

Initial outreach and validation prioritize:

- Rugby.
- Hockey.
- Football.
- Basketball.

These sports are strong entry markets because:

- Clubs have strong identity.
- Youth categories are structured.
- Development values are important.
- Coaches have strong influence.
- Long-term player development is valued.

---

# Broader Sports Coverage

The product is not restricted to those markets. It can serve volleyball, tennis, athletics, swimming and any other sport where structured reflection and athlete development create value.

---

# Club Adoption Model

Implementation should be progressive.

---

# Phase 1

## Pilot

Objective:

Validate adoption and value.

Activities:

- Select categories.
- Train coaches.
- Invite athletes.
- Collect feedback.

---

# Phase 2

## Adoption

Objective:

Create regular usage.

Activities:

- Integrate evaluations into routines.
- Encourage athlete reflection.
- Use insights in conversations.

---

# Phase 3

## Organization Integration

Objective:

Make Sentiq part of club methodology.

Activities:

- Expand categories.
- Include more coaches.
- Create development processes.

# Club Risks

---

## Technology Without Adoption

A platform only creates value when people use it.

---

## Excessive Complexity

Implementation must be simple.

---

## Coach Resistance

Sentiq should reduce friction, not create additional administrative work.
